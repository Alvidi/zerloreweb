ZEROLORE - QUICK PLAY RULES

Use any miniatures. Agree on a Value. Start playing.

Before you start

This is everything you need to know before reading the rules:

  • Miniatures: use any models you have. It does not matter where they come from.
  • Value: each unit has a points cost called Value. It is used to build your army and contest objectives.
  • Actions: each time you activate a unit, it can perform 2 different actions.
  • Objectives: there are 3 on the table. Whoever controls more at the end wins.
  • Dice: use six-sided dice (D6). A roll of 6 is always a critical hit.

Game size

Before starting, agree on the total Value per player:

FormatValue per player
Skirmish50 – 100
Squad150 – 250

Game modes:

  • Skirmish: each miniature is an independent unit.
  • Squads: several miniatures form one unit. Damage is applied to the squad as a whole. When its Lives reach 0, it is destroyed. The player chooses which miniature is removed when the damage equals its Lives.

Setup

  1. Place 3 objectives on the table.
  1. Deploy in opposite zones.
  1. Decide who starts by rolling 1D6. The highest result activates first.

Measuring distances

All distances are measured in inches ("), from base to base, from the nearest point of each miniature. You may measure at any time.

Line of sight

A unit can shoot another unit if it can see any part of its miniature from the attacker’s position. If it is completely hidden, it cannot be targeted at range.

Turn structure

Players alternate activations, one unit at a time. When all units have been activated, the round ends.

Each unit can perform 2 different actions per activation; it cannot repeat the same action twice.

ActionCost

Movement

The unit may move up to its MOV value in inches in any direction. It cannot end its move within 1" of an enemy unit, except when charging.

Shooting

Every ranged attack follows this sequence:

1. Choose target
A visible enemy unit within the weapon’s range.

2. Determine attacks
Roll as many dice as indicated by the weapon’s Attacks value.

3. Roll hits
Each die equal to or above the weapon’s Hits value scores a hit. A 6 is always critical.

4. Roll saves
The defender rolls one die per hit received. Each result equal to or above its Save blocks one hit. Only a defensive 6 can block a critical hit.

5. Apply damage
Each unblocked hit inflicts the weapon’s base damage. Each unblocked critical inflicts critical damage. When a unit loses all its Lives, it is removed from play.

Example: Tactical Infantry fires with a Battle Rifle (2D, Hits +4, Damage 1/2). It rolls 2 dice and gets 4 and 6: two hits, with the 6 being critical. The defender rolls 2 save dice and blocks the normal hit. It takes the critical: 2 damage.

Ready

A unit in Ready state adopts a defensive stance.

  • If it is shot at before its next activation, it may immediately perform a reaction shot against the attacker. Resolve the enemy attack first, then the reaction shot.
  • If it is charged: combat is resolved by Speed; the unit with higher Speed attacks first. The unit in Ready may attack or counterattack even if it has no actions left.

Sprint

The unit spends 2 actions. Roll 1D6 and add the result to its MOV for that move. During a Sprint it cannot attack, unless a rule says otherwise.

Charge

To start melee, a unit must Charge (2 actions). Roll 2D6; this is the maximum number of inches it can move toward the target.

  • If it does not reach the target, the charge fails, the unit remains in place, and the 2 actions are still spent.
  • If the charge is successful, the unit moves into contact with the target and automatically includes a melee attack.

Melee

This occurs when two units are in base contact. Both become engaged; while engaged, they may only use melee actions or Retreat.

Attack order:

  1. The unit that charged attacks first.
  1. If no one charged, the unit with higher Speed attacks first.
  1. If Speed is tied, roll 1D6.

Sequence:

  1. Attack: roll the melee weapon’s Attack dice. Results of 1 and 2 miss. The rest hit. A 6 is critical.
  1. Saves: same as in shooting.
  1. Counterattack: if the defending unit survives, it must counterattack (if it has actions left).

If both survive, they remain engaged until one is destroyed or retreats.

Objectives and victory

The game lasts 4-5 rounds.

At the end of each round:

  • Add the Value of your units in contact with each objective.
  • The player with the highest Value controls that objective.

Whoever controls the most objectives wins by adding control points at the end of the game.

General rule

If a situation is not covered here, resolve it with common sense and mutual agreement. If there is no consensus, roll 1D6 and continue.

For full rules on cover, terrain, unit types, doctrines, and Conquest mode, see the Advanced Rulebook.
