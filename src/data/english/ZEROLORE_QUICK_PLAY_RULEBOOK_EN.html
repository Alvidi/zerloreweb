ZEROLORE - QUICK-PLAY RULEBOOK

Use any miniature. Agree on a Value. Start playing.

Before You Start

This is everything you need to know before reading the rules:

  • Miniatures: use any figures you have. It doesn't matter where they come from.
  • Value: each unit has a points cost called Value. It is used to build your army and to contest objectives.
  • Actions: each time you activate a unit it can perform 2 different actions.
  • Objectives: there are 3 on the table. Whoever controls the most at the end wins.
  • Dice: six-sided dice (D6) are used. A result of 6 is always a critical hit.

Game Size

Before starting, agree on the total Value per player:

FormatValue per player
Skirmish50 – 100
Squad150 – 250

Game Modes:

  • Skirmish: each miniature is an independent unit.
  • Squads: varias miniaturas forman una sola unidad. El daño se aplica a la escuadra en conjunto — cuando sus Vidas llegan a 0 es eliminada. El jugador decide qué miniatura retira cuando el daño equivale a sus Vidas.

Setup

  1. Place 3 objectives on the table.
  1. Deploy in opposite zones.
  1. Determine who goes first by rolling 1D6 — the highest result activates first.

Measuring Distances

All distances are measured in inches (") de base to base, from the nearest point of each miniature. Distances may be measured at any time.

Line of Sight

A unit may shoot at another if it can see any part of its miniature from the attacker's position. If completely hidden, it cannot be attacked at range.

Turn Structure

Players alternate activations, one unit at a time. When all units have been activated, the round ends.

Each unit may perform 2 different actions per activation — it cannot repeat the same action twice.

ActionCost

Shoot1 action

Sprint2 actions
Charge2 actions

Movement

The unit may move up to its MOV value in inches in any direction. It cannot end its move within 1" of an enemy unit, except when charging.

Shooting

Every ranged attack follows this sequence:

1. Choose Target
A visible enemy unit within the weapon's range.

2. Determine Attacks
Roll as many dice as indicated by the weapon's Attacks value.

3. Roll Hits
Each die equal to or greater than the weapon's Hits value is a hit. A 6 is always a critical.

4. Roll Saves
The defender rolls as many dice as hits received. Each result equal to or greater than their Save blocks a hit. Only a defensive 6 can block a critical hit.

5. Apply Damage
Each unblocked hit inflicts the weapon's base damage . Each unblocked critical inflicts the weapon's critical damage. When a unit loses all its Lives, it is removed from the game.

Example: La Infantería Táctica dispara con el Fusil de Batalla (2D, Impactos +4, Daño 1/2). Lanza 2 dados y saca 4 y 6 — dos impactos, el 6 es crítico. El defensor lanza 2 dados de salvación y bloquea el impacto normal. Recibe el crítico: 2 puntos de daño.

Ready

A unit in Ready state adopts a defensive stance.

  • If shot at before its next activation: it may immediately make a return shot against the attacker. The enemy attack is resolved first, then the return shot.
  • If charged: combat is resolved by Speed — the unit with higher Speed attacks first. The Ready unit may attack or counterattack even if it has no actions remaining.

Sprint

The unit uses 2 actions. Roll 1D6 and add the result to its MOV for that move. During a Sprint it cannot attack, unless a rule states otherwise.

Charge

To initiate close combat, the unit must Charge (2 actions). Roll 2D6 — that is the maximum inches it can move towards the target.

  • Si does not reach the target: the charge fails, the unit stays in place and the 2 actions are still spent.
  • If the charge is successful: the unit is placed in contact with the target and automatically includes a close combat attack.

Close Combat

Occurs when two units are in base contact. Both become locked — while engaged, they may only use close combat or Retreat actions.

Attack order:

  1. The unit that has charged attacks first.
  1. If neither charged, the unit with the higher Speed.
  1. If Speed is tied, roll 1D6.

Sequence:

  1. Attack: roll the Attack dice of the close combat weapon. Results of 1 and 2 are misses. All others are hits. A 6 is a critical.
  1. Saves: same as in shooting.
  1. Counterattack: if the defending unit survives, it must counterattack (if it has actions remaining).

If both survive, they remain locked until one is eliminated or retreats.

Objectives and Victory

The game lasts 4–5 rounds.

At the end of each round:

  • Add up the Valor of your units in contact with each objective.
  • The player with the highest Value controls that objective.

The player who controls the most objectives wins adding up control points at the end of the game.

General Rule

If a situation is not covered here, resolve it with common sense and mutual agreement. If there is no consensus, roll 1D6 and continue.

For full rules on cover, terrain, unit types, doctrines and Conquest mode, refer to the Advanced Rulebook.
