ZEROLORE - QUICK PLAY RULEBOOK

Use any miniature. Agree on a Value. Start playing.

Before starting

This is everything you need to know before reading the rules:

  • Miniatures: use any figure you have. It doesn't matter where they come from.
  • Value: Each unit has a cost in points called Value. It is used to build your army and to contest objectives.
  • Actions: every time you activate a unit it can do 2 different actions.
  • Objectives: there are 3 on the table. Whoever controls the most at the end wins.
  • Dice: 6-sided dice (D6) are used. A result of 6 is always a critical hit.

Starting size

Before you begin, agree on the Total Value per player:

FormatValue per player
Skirmish50 – 100
Squad150 – 250

Game modes:

  • Skirmish: Each model is an independent unit.
  • Squads: several miniatures form a single unit. The damage is applied to the squad as a whole — when its Life reaches 0 it is eliminated. The player decides which model he removes when the damage equals his Lives.

Preparation

  1. Place 3 objectives on the table.
  1. Deploy in opposite areas.
  1. Determine who goes first by rolling 1D6 — the highest result activates first.

Distance measurement

All distances are measured in inches (") from base to base, from the closest point of each miniature. It can be measured at any time.

Line of sight

A unit can fire at another if it can see any part of its model from the attacker's position. If it is completely hidden, it cannot be attacked from a distance.

Turn Structure

Players alternate activations, one unit at a time. When all units have been activated, the round ends.

Each unit can perform 2 different actions per activation — it cannot repeat the same action twice.

Movement

The unit can move up to its MOV value in inches in any direction. You cannot end your movement within 1" of an enemy unit, except when charging.

Shooting

Every ranged attack follows this sequence:

1. Choose target: an enemy unit visible and within weapon range.

2. Determine attacks: roll as many dice as the weapon's Attacks value indicates.

3. Roll to hit: each die equal to or greater than the weapon's Hits value is a hit. A 6 is always critical.

4. Roll saves: the defender rolls as many dice as hits received. Each result equal to or greater than its Save blocks one hit. Only a defensive 6 can block a critical hit.

5. Apply damage: each unblocked hit deals the weapon's base damage. Each unblocked critical deals critical damage. When a unit loses all its Lives, it is removed from the game.

Example: The Tactical Infantry fires with the Battle Rifle (2D, Hits +4, Damage 1/2). Roll 2 dice and roll 4 and 6 — two hits, 6 is critical. The defender rolls 2 save dice and blocks the normal hit. Receives the critical: 2 points of damage.

Ready

A unit in Ready status adopts a defensive posture.

  • If you are shot before your next activation: You can immediately fire a return shot at the attacker. First the enemy attack is resolved, then the response shot.
  • If charged: Combat is resolved by Speed ​​— the unit with the highest Speed ​​attacks first. The Ready unit can attack or counterattack even if it has no actions left.

Sprint

The unit consumes 2 actions. Roll 1D6 and add the result to your MOV for that move. During a Sprint you cannot attack, unless a rule indicates otherwise.

Charge

To start a melee combat, the unit must Charge (2 actions). Roll 2D6 — that's the maximum number of inches you can travel toward the target.

  • If it misses the target: the charge fails, the unit remains in its position and the 2 actions are consumed equally.
  • If the charge is successful: The unit is placed in contact with the target and automatically includes a melee combat attack.

Melee

Occurs when two units are in base contact. Both become locked — while locked, they can only use melee combat or Retreat actions.

Attack order:

  1. The charging unit attacks first.
  1. If no one charged, attack the one with the highest Speed ​​first.
  1. If there is a Speed ​​tie, roll 1D6.

Sequence:

  1. Attack: Roll the Attack dice for the melee combat weapon. The results of 1 and 2 are failures. The rest impact. A 6 is critical.
  1. Saves: same as shooting.
  1. Counterattack: If the defending unit survives, it must counterattack (if it has any actions left).

If both survive, they remain locked until one is eliminated or retreats.

Objectives and victory

The game lasts 4-5 rounds.

At the end of each round:

  • Add up the Value of your units in contact with each target.
  • The player with the highest Value controls that objective.

Whoever controls the most objectives wins by adding the control points at the end of the game.

General Rule

If a situation is not covered here, resolve it with common sense and mutual agreement. If there is no consensus, roll 1D6 and continue.

For complete rules on cover, terrain, unit types, doctrines, and Conquest mode, see the Advanced Rulebook.
