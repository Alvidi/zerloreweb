Siege

Siege Mode is an asymmetrical format where one player defends a fortified position while the other attempts to conquer it. Ideal for narrative and cinematographic games.

Roles

Before starting, the players agree who is the Attacker and who is the Defender.

army value

Players freely agree on the Total Value of the game. The Defender builds his army with 25% less Value than the Attacker to compensate for the defensive advantage.

Example:

  • Value Game 100 → Attacker 100, Defender 75
  • Value Game 200 → Attacker 200, Defender 150

Preparation

  1. The Defender chooses his deployment zone — an end of the table with defensive scenery such as barricades and turrets is recommended.
  1. The Defender places 2 objectives in his zone. Each target must be 9" or more from the other and be at least 3" in length.
  1. The Defender deploys his units, turrets and barricades.
  1. The Attacker deploys at the opposite end.

Barricades

The Defender can place any piece of scenery as a barricade. Barricades provide partial cover to units in contact with them and cannot be destroyed.

Turrets

The Defender can include turrets in his list as normal units. They activate like any other unit during the Activation Phase. There are two types available:

Duration

The game lasts a minimum of 7 turns, always in an odd number. Players can agree on more turns before starting.

Victory

  • The Attacker wins if he conquers both objectives before the turns end.
  • The Defender wins if he retains at least 1 objective at the end of the last turn.
