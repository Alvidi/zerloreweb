# Conquest

Conquest Mode is the simplest format to start playing ZeroLore. Two or four players in teams compete for control of the battlefield through the occupation of strategic zones and the destruction of enemy units.

## Setup

1. Players agree on army size.
2. Objectives are placed on the table, always in an odd number with a minimum of 3. It is recommended that each objective be at least 6" in diameter, although size, shape, and position are free as long as both players agree.
3. Deployment is carried out following the standard rules.

---

## Duration

The game lasts a minimum of 5 turns. Players agree on the exact number before starting.

---

## Objective Control

An objective belongs to the player who has at least one unit or squad inside it at the end of the turn. For it to count as controlled, at least **50% of the objective area** must be occupied by friendly units. If that player removes all their units, the objective becomes **neutral** until someone occupies it again; the enemy does not need to enter it, it is enough to abandon it.

---

## Scoring

At the end of each turn, points are added from two sources:

**Objective control:** each controlled objective grants **5 points**. Players may agree on a different value before the game, or assign different values to each objective.

**Unit elimination:** destroying an enemy unit or squad grants points equal to its Value. Each elimination counts even if that unit later reappears.

---

### Secondary Missions

In the first Initiative Phase, each player draws **2 secondary missions** at random from the list. They are secret and are not shown to the opponent until completed. When one is completed, it is revealed immediately and the points are scored in the next End of Turn phase. If a mission has not been completed by the end of the game, it scores nothing.

| Mission        | Description                                                                 | Points |
| -------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------ |
| Decapitation   | Destroy the opponent's highest-value unit or squad.                         | +6 pts |
| Scorched Earth | Destroy 2 enemy units or squads in the same turn.                           | +4 pts |
| Flanking       | Have a unit or squad in the enemy deployment zone at the end of a turn.     | +4 pts |
| Total Control  | Control all objectives at the end of the same turn.                         | +6 pts |
| Big Game Hunt  | Destroy an Elite, Vehicle, Monster, or Titan unit or squad.                 | +5 pts |
| Forward Push   | Have units or squads controlling 2 different objectives at the same time at the end of a turn. | +4 pts |
| Last One Standing | Have a unit or squad that has remained on the table from turn 1 to the end of the game. | +4 pts |
| Ambush         | Destroy an enemy unit with a unit that has not yet been activated that turn. | +3 pts |

---

## Victory

The player with the most points at the end of the last turn wins. In case of a tie, the player who controls more objectives at that moment wins.

---

> The included game modes, Conquest and Dominion, are the starting point for anyone who wants to begin playing without complications. But ZeroLore does not end there. The core rules are a toolbox: you can use them to design your own missions, campaigns with consequences, custom scenario rules, or any format that suits your table. If you and your opponent agree, anything is valid.
