# ZEROLORE - Quick Rulebook

*Use any miniature. Agree on a Value. Start playing.*

---

## Before You Begin

Everything you need to know before reading the rules:

- **Miniatures:** use any figures you have. It doesn't matter where they come from.
- **Value:** each unit has a point cost called Value. It's used to build your army and to contest objectives.
- **Actions:** each time you activate a unit it can take 2 different actions.
- **Objectives:** there are 3 on the table. Whoever controls the most at the end wins.
- **Dice:** six-sided dice (D6) are used. A result of 6 is always a critical hit.

---

## Game Size

Before starting, agree on the total Value per player:

| Format     | Value per player |
|------------|------------------|
| Skirmish   | 30 – 80          |

In skirmish mode each miniature is an independent unit.

---

## Setup

- Place 3 objectives on the table.
- Deploy in opposite zones.
- Determine who goes first by rolling 1D6 — the highest result activates first.

---

## Measuring Distances

All distances are measured in inches (") from base to base, from the closest point of each miniature. You may measure at any time.

---

## Line of Sight

A unit can shoot at another if it can see any part of the target miniature from the attacker's position. If the target is completely hidden, it cannot be attacked at range.

---

## Turn Structure

Players alternate activations, one unit at a time. When all units have been activated, the round ends.

Each unit may take 2 different actions per activation — it cannot repeat the same action twice.

| Action        | Cost      |
|---------------|-----------|
| Move          | 1 action  |
| Shoot         | 1 action  |
| Ready         | 2 actions |
| Sprint        | 2 actions |
| Charge        | 2 actions |
| Melee Attack  | 2 actions |

---

## Movement

The unit may move up to its MOV value in inches in any direction. It cannot end its movement within 1" of an enemy unit, except when charging.

---

## Shooting

Every ranged attack follows this sequence:

1. **Choose target** — a visible enemy unit within the weapon's range.
2. **Determine attacks** — roll as many dice as the weapon's Attacks value indicates.
3. **Roll to hit** — each die equal to or above the weapon's Hit value scores a hit. A 6 is always a critical hit.
4. **Roll saves** — the defender rolls as many dice as hits received. Each result equal to or above their Save value blocks a hit. Only a defensive 6 can block a critical hit.
5. **Apply damage** — each unblocked hit deals the weapon's base damage. Each unblocked critical deals critical damage. When a unit loses all its Wounds, it is removed from the game.

*Example: Tactical Infantry shoots with the Battle Rifle (2D, Hit 4+, Damage 1/2). They roll 2 dice and get 4 and 6 — two hits, the 6 is critical. The defender rolls 2 save dice and blocks the normal hit. They take the critical: 2 damage.*

---

## Ready

A unit in Ready state adopts a defensive posture.

- **If shot at:** it may immediately fire back at the attacker. The enemy attack resolves first, then the return fire.
- **If charged:** combat is resolved by Speed — the unit with higher Speed attacks first. The Ready unit may attack or counterattack even if it has no actions left.

---

## Sprint

The unit spends 2 actions. Roll 1D6 and add the result to its MOV for that movement. During a Sprint the unit cannot attack, unless a rule states otherwise.

---

## Charge

To initiate melee combat, the unit must Charge (2 actions). Roll 2D6 — that is the maximum distance it can move toward the target.

- **If it doesn't reach:** the charge fails, the unit stays in place and the 2 actions are spent.
- **If the charge succeeds:** the unit is placed in base contact with the target and automatically includes a melee attack.

---

## Melee Combat

Occurs when two units are in base contact. Both become locked — while locked, they may only use melee or Retreat actions.

**Attack order:**

- The charging unit attacks first.
- If no one charged, the unit with higher Speed attacks first.
- If Speed is tied, roll 1D6.

**Sequence:**

- **Attack:** roll the melee weapon's Attack dice. Results of 1 and 2 are misses. The rest are hits. A 6 is critical.
- **Saves:** same as shooting.
- **Counterattack:** if the defending unit survives, it must counterattack (if it has actions remaining).

If both survive, they remain locked until one is destroyed or retreats.

---

## Objectives and Victory

The game lasts 4–5 rounds.

At the end of each round, add up the Value of your units in contact with each objective. The player with the highest Value controls that objective. The player who controls the most objectives at the end of the game wins.

---

## General Rule

If a situation is not covered here, resolve it with common sense and mutual agreement. If there is no consensus, roll 1D6 and continue.

For full rules on cover, terrain, unit types (including **Flyers**), doctrines, Squad mode and more, see the Advanced Rulebook.
