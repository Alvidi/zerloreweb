ZeroLore lets you play any kind of battle you have in mind: you can create your own missions, scenarios, and campaigns using the rulebook as a base. What follows is the **basic mission** — if you want to play straight away with what ZeroLore offers, start here.

---

# Game Setup

Before starting, players agree on:

- The **command posts** to place on the table and where — they are used to deploy units and are conquest objectives.
- The **number of turns** — recommended: **5**.
- Each player's **total army value**.
- The **game mode** — Skirmish or Grand Battle.
- The **mission** being played. Each mission defines its own objectives.

---

# Basic Mission

_Everything counts. Everything matters._

The rulebook tells you how each thing works; the mission tells you **what you're fighting for**.
This is ZeroLore's core mission: it works the same in Skirmish and Grand Battle.

## Setup

- There must always be at least one command post (in this case the Headquarters) under each player's control at the start. The rest may be neutral.
- If players build the map freely, they decide the distribution of posts between them and designate their own Headquarters before the battle begins.

## How to Win

**Immediate victory** — the battle ends on the spot if:

- You capture the **enemy Headquarters**.
- The opponent is left **with no units** on the battlefield (once their first units have been deployed).

**Points victory** — if no HQ falls, the player with the most points at the end of the battle wins. In case of a tie, the player controlling more command posts wins, or who has destroyed more units if command posts are equal.

## How to Score

At the end of each turn, each player scores:

- **+10 points** for each command post they control, including their HQ.
- **The points value** of each enemy unit destroyed during that turn. Each unit only grants points **the first time** it is destroyed — units that return to the battlefield (respawn, resurrection, or other effects) do not grant points again.

## Suggested Skirmish Maps

These maps use a **36" x 36"** battlefield and circular **3"** command posts. Each player starts in the colour of their **Headquarters**. Black command posts are neutral command posts.

The maps are designed for up to **4 players**. If only 2 players are playing, they use Headquarters **1** and **2**; Headquarters **3** and **4** are treated as neutral command posts.

## Suggested Grand Battle Maps

These maps use **46" x 46"** or **72" x 48"** battlefields and circular **6"** command posts. The corner map spreads command posts in a steadier layout; the exposed HQ map moves the Headquarters closer to the centre to force early pressure.

The maps are designed for up to **4 players**. If only 2 players are playing, they use Headquarters **1** and **2**; Headquarters **3** and **4** are treated as neutral command posts.
